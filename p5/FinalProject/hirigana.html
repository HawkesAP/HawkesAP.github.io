Japanese Hirigana

The Japanese alphabet consists of hiragana, katakana, and kanji. Hiragana and katakana are the same letters phonetically but wrote differently to differentiate between native Japanese words and foreign words, people, and concepts. Japanese also use kanji, which are Chinese letters that they use along with hiragana for native Japanese words.
